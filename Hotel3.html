Discover the World of Dosa

Dosa is a traditional South Indian dish that has become popular across India and beyond. Itâ€™s a type of thin, crispy pancake made from fermented rice and lentil batter. Known for its versatility, dosa can be enjoyed plain or with a variety of fillings and toppings. Its origins trace back to ancient India, and it's cherished for its distinctive taste and texture. Here, we explore the different types of dosa, each offering a unique flavor and experience.

Plain Dosa

A classic, crispy dosa made from a simple fermented batter. It has a mild tanginess from fermentation and a delightful crunch.

Masala Dosa

Filled with a spiced potato mixture, this dosa offers a savory and slightly spicy experience with a blend of textures.

Rava Dosa

Made from semolina, this dosa is thin and crispy, with a hint of cumin and black pepper for extra flavor.

Onion Dosa

Featuring caramelized onions and green chilies, this dosa combines a sweet and savory flavor profile with its crisp texture.

Cheese Dosa

A modern twist with melted cheese, this dosa is rich and creamy, perfect for cheese lovers.

Paper Dosa

This super thin, wafer-like dosa is known for its extra crispiness and large size, providing a satisfying crunch.

Set Dosa

Soft and thick, these dosas are served in sets of three, offering a mild and spongy texture, ideal with various chutneys and sambar.

Pesarattu

Made from green gram, this dosa has an earthy flavor and is commonly served with ginger chutney.
